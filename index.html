Victor Awosiji

Writing Talks Research Media
Victor Awosiji is a PhD researcher in Earth & Planetary Sciences at Stanford, where he works on AI-driven exploration for natural hydrogen. He developed the Blind Hydrogen System, a first-principles framework for finding hydrogen underground without surface clues.

His work sits at the intersection of deep learning, geophysics, and energy—building transformer architectures that fuse gravity, magnetic, and well-log data to predict where geological hydrogen sits beneath the Earth's surface. He convened the Natural Hydrogen session at AGU 2024 and has been invited to speak at conferences across the US and internationally.

Previously, he developed hydrogen exploration methodologies at California Resources Corporation and Aramco Americas that were adopted across their operations in the US and Saudi Arabia. He holds an M.Sc. with Distinction and a B.Sc. with First-Class Honors, both in geological sciences.

He thinks about energy at civilization scale, the role of AI in the physical world, and what it means to build things that matter.
